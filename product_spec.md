# dewdrop

> the social platform where events are the content.

---

## the problem

there are plenty of tools to *create* an event page. there's no good way to *discover what your friends are doing this weekend*.

events live in instagram stories that vanish in 24 hours, whatsapp groups where they get buried, and posters nobody photographs. you hear about the good ones the day after.

---

## what exists today

### luma
beautiful event pages. great ticketing. organizers love it. but attendees open it, rsvp, close it, and never come back. there's no feed, no social graph, no reason to check the app on a tuesday afternoon. it's a tool, not a platform.

### meetup
built in 2002. acquired by bending spoons in 2024. organizer fees hiked to $45/month. pay-to-skip waitlists. bots everywhere. organizers are leaving. the ui looks like it was last updated during the obama administration.

### partiful
fun, gen z, colorful. but you need an app download just to rsvp. there's zero discovery — if you're not invited, you don't know it exists. it's a digital invitation card, not a platform.

### eventbrite
corporate ticketing. no personality, no social features, no reason for a 22-year-old to open it. nobody browses eventbrite for fun.

---

## what none of them have

- a feed that shows what your friends are going to
- a reason for attendees to come back every day
- gamification — xp, tiers, leaderboards
- ai-generated event posters
- event creation that's free and takes 30 seconds

---

## what dewdrop is

dewdrop is instagram but for events. you open it, you see what your people are doing, you join.

**social feed** — your feed isn't algorithmic recommendations. it's "ali is going to rooftop jazz night" and "3 of your friends are at the street food festival." real people, real plans.

**xp & tiers** — every rsvp, every event you create, every event you actually show up to earns xp. you level up: newcomer → tide rider → storm chaser → wave maker → ocean. the leaderboard resets weekly. there's always a reason to come back.

**ai posters** — nobody wants to open canva at midnight. type a title, pick a vibe, get a poster. done.

**free for creators** — luma charges $59/month. meetup charges $45/month. dewdrop charges nothing to create events. ever.

**beautiful** — editorial design, gradient posters, dark mode, playfair display headings. this thing looks like a magazine, not a spreadsheet.

---

## the core loop

```
see what friends are doing
        ↓
      rsvp
        ↓
     attend
        ↓
    earn xp
        ↓
    level up
        ↓
 create your own event
        ↓
 your friends see it
        ↓
     (repeat)
```

---

## revenue

**free forever for organizers.** that's non-negotiable. the money comes from:

- **ticket commission** — 5-10% cut on paid event ticket sales
- **premium membership** — unlimited ai posters, analytics, featured badge, profile themes
- **event sponsorship** — pay to be featured in the discover banner
- **promoted events** — boost visibility in the feed
